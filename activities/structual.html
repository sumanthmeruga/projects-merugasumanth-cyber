Structural Tags

Structural tags in HTML are used to define the organization and hierarchy of content within a web page, providing semantic meaning and improving accessibility and search engine optimization.

    What goes in the header?

    A header holds the title, logo, and navigation links at the top of a page or section.

    What goes in the Nav? Can the Nav go in the header? Does it have to go in the header? Does the Nav contain links to another website?

    Navigation menus — usually a list of links that help users move around a site.

    What goes in Main?

    The holds the page’s main content that is unique and central to that page.

    What goes in the Footer?

    The holds closing content like copyright, contact info, site credits, or extra links.
